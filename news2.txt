Iranian state media are doing their best to undercut a triumphant moment for US President Donald Trump after the rescue of the missing crew member from a downed fighter jet.

Trump said late Saturday US time that the airman had been recovered after the US military had “sent dozens of aircraft, armed with the most lethal weapons” to retrieve him. “He sustained injuries, but he will be just fine,” Trump said.

As an analyst told us a short time ago, the rescue prevented the crew member becoming a “bargaining chip” for Tehran.

How Tehran is reporting the rescue: But the Tasnim News Agency meanwhile claimed “several enemy American aircraft in the southern Isfahan region were destroyed by the warriors of Islam, and the pilot rescue operation failed,” citing Iran’s military headquarters.

“The invading enemy aircraft in southern Isfahan, including 2 Black Hawk helicopters and one military transport aircraft C-130, were hit and are now burning,” it said.

Several official Iranian outlets published video purporting to show the smoking wreckage of the aircraft in a desert area.

CNN is unable to verify its authenticity and has reached out to the US military for comment.
